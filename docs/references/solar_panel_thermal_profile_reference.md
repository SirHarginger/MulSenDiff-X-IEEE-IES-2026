# Solar Panel Thermal Profile Reference

## Defect pattern
Solar-panel inspections may activate internal thermal cues even for acceptable baseline samples. The key distinction is whether the suspected region stays compact and consistent with known benign thermal structure.

## Sensor signature
When thermal-internal cues are present, compare hotspot area, compactness, and mean hotspot intensity against the normal panel profile. Cross-modal inconsistency alone is not enough to confirm a true defect.

## Likely root causes
- Benign thermal concentration around expected conductive paths
- Local measurement variation near connectors or panel features
- True internal heating only if the compact cue expands or intensifies abnormally

## Inspection checks
- Compare the suspected region against a trusted normal thermal reference
- Verify whether hotspot compactness remains within the expected pattern
- Escalate if the region broadens, intensifies, or repeats outside the known baseline zone

## Corrective action
Keep the sample under monitored review if the signature matches the benign baseline. Escalate for thermal reinspection if internal-heating cues intensify or spread beyond the expected compact region.

## Trust notes
Use this reference when solar-panel detections emphasize internal thermal cues and cross-modal inconsistency but still require confirmation against the normal baseline.
