# Light Housing Break Reference

## Defect pattern
Broken light samples usually appear as compact localized faults with strong global drift and mixed cross-modal evidence. The detector often treats the region as a high-severity compact anomaly.

## Sensor signature
Look for a tight region with strong residual disagreement and cross-modal inconsistency. Geometry or thermal support may be weak locally, so confirm the anomaly against housing continuity and known part silhouette.

## Likely root causes
- Cracked housing after impact
- Broken edge near lens or enclosure seam
- Local misfit introduced during handling or packaging

## Inspection checks
- Inspect the housing edge around the dominant region
- Compare enclosure outline with a known-good part
- Check for chipped or displaced material at the break boundary

## Corrective action
Remove the unit from outbound handling, verify enclosure integrity, and review impact or packaging events if multiple lights fail in the same pattern.

## Trust notes
This reference is suited to compact light anomalies where the detector emphasizes score-basis evidence and global descriptor drift.
