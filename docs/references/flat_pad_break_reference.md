# Flat Pad Break Reference

## Defect pattern
Broken flat-pad samples tend to concentrate in a compact region while the detector reports strong global descriptor drift. The anomaly may remain visually subtle but still break the learned normal pad profile.

## Sensor signature
Expect compact cross-modal inconsistency with weak local thermal and geometric support. Confirm the region against pad edge continuity and local material breakage.

## Likely root causes
- Edge fracture during handling
- Local material tear or chip-out
- Compression damage that changed the expected outline

## Inspection checks
- Inspect the compact region for chipped edges or broken corners
- Compare the pad outline with a known normal part
- Check whether the affected area matches a stress concentration point

## Corrective action
Remove the damaged pad, verify fixture pressure and handling contact points, and review whether breakage clusters near a repeated edge location.

## Trust notes
This reference supports compact flat-pad anomalies dominated by detector residuals and global descriptor drift.
