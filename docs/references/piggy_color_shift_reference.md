# Piggy Color Shift Reference

## Defect pattern
Piggy color anomalies often behave as compact appearance faults with strong score-basis disagreement and descriptor drift. Local geometry may remain weakly affected while the overall appearance profile shifts away from normal.

## Sensor signature
Look for a compact region with cross-modal inconsistency and strong residual response. Thermal evidence is often weak, so confirmation depends on localized appearance mismatch and deviation from the learned global profile.

## Likely root causes
- Pigment inconsistency during finishing
- Surface contamination or coating mismatch
- Local discoloration after thermal or chemical exposure

## Inspection checks
- Inspect the highlighted region for color or tone deviation
- Compare the surface finish against a known-good piggy sample
- Check whether the anomaly is localized or spreading beyond the compact region

## Corrective action
Separate the part for finish review, inspect pigment or coating controls, and look for contamination or exposure events in the upstream process.

## Trust notes
This reference is intended for compact piggy appearance anomalies dominated by score-basis evidence and global descriptor drift.
