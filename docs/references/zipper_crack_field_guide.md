# Zipper Crack and Separation Field Guide

## Defect pattern
Cracks around zipper teeth or seam attachment points usually appear as compact structural breaks. In shared inspection, these faults often surface as strong residual disagreement with moderate geometric irregularity.

## Sensor signature
Look for cross-modal inconsistency around a compact region. Geometry should be checked for local tooth displacement, edge separation, or seam misalignment even when thermal evidence is weak.

## Likely root causes
- Mechanical overstress during stitching or attachment
- Repeated bending that weakens the zipper tooth base
- Local seam pull-out or tooth misalignment

## Inspection checks
- Verify tooth continuity and edge alignment
- Check whether the compact anomaly sits near the seam or tooth root
- Compare the dominant region against known straight-line zipper geometry

## Corrective action
If the crack is localized, isolate the defective zipper segment and inspect upstream attachment tooling. If repeated samples show the same compact seam-adjacent fault, review stitching force and alignment setup.

## Trust notes
This reference is intended for detector-grounded follow-up on zipper crack findings where cross-modal inconsistency and geometric change dominate the evidence.
