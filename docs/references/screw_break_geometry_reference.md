# Screw Break Geometry Reference

## Defect pattern
Broken screw samples usually appear as compact structural disruptions with strong descriptor drift relative to the normal thread profile. The detector often emphasizes compact residual disagreement plus geometry-sensitive deviation.

## Sensor signature
Expect cross-modal inconsistency with a tight anomalous region around the damaged thread or broken shaft area. Thermal evidence may remain weak while the global descriptor branch reinforces the anomaly.

## Likely root causes
- Excess fastening torque or mechanical overload
- Material fatigue at the thread root
- Local shaft fracture after repeated stress cycles

## Inspection checks
- Confirm whether the dominant region lies on the thread path or shaft edge
- Review thread continuity against a known-good screw
- Check for brittle fracture signs or chipped edges

## Corrective action
Remove the defective screw from the line, inspect fastening torque settings, and review whether the break location is consistent with thread-root overstress or handling impact.

## Trust notes
Use this guide when the detector highlights a compact screw anomaly with strong global drift and mixed cross-modal evidence.
