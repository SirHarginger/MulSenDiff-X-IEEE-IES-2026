# Button Cell Inspection Profile Reference

## Defect pattern
Button-cell inspections can show localized score excursions even when the part is ultimately normal. Compact anomalies should therefore be interpreted against trusted baseline handling notes before escalation.

## Sensor signature
Cross-modal inconsistency may appear in compact regions because of reflective surfaces and local baseline variation. Treat global descriptor drift cautiously and confirm whether the region corresponds to a benign normal-profile artifact.

## Likely root causes
- Reflective variation on the metal surface
- Small alignment or lighting mismatch in the captured view
- Benign local contrast change that does not indicate true damage

## Inspection checks
- Compare the dominant region against a normal button-cell reference
- Confirm whether the highlighted area corresponds to a known reflective or alignment artifact
- Escalate only if the compact anomaly is accompanied by clear physical damage

## Corrective action
If physical damage is not visible, retain the sample as a likely normal case and continue monitoring. If the same compact region repeatedly escalates, review lighting, placement, and baseline normalization.

## Trust notes
This reference is appropriate for button-cell inspections where compact detector signals require normal-profile verification rather than immediate rejection.
