# Cube Hole and Missing Material Reference

## Defect pattern
Cube hole defects usually present as compact missing-material anomalies. The detector often shows high global drift because a local cavity changes the expected object profile significantly.

## Sensor signature
Expect compact residual disagreement with cross-modal inconsistency around the missing-material region. Geometry may appear weaker than expected locally, so compare the region against known cube continuity.

## Likely root causes
- Missing material after molding or machining
- Local punch-through or cavity defect
- Edge loss caused by handling damage

## Inspection checks
- Inspect the dominant region for true material absence
- Compare local faces and edges against a normal cube
- Verify whether the cavity is internal, through-hole, or edge-adjacent

## Corrective action
Reject the part, inspect the forming process for incomplete fill or punch-through, and review whether repeated holes appear at the same face location.

## Trust notes
Use this guide when the detector highlights a compact cube anomaly with strong descriptor drift and cross-modal inconsistency.
