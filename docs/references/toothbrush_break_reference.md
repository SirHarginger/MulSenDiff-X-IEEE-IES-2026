# Toothbrush Break Reference

## Defect pattern
Broken toothbrush parts usually manifest as compact structural anomalies with strong deviation from the normal shape profile. The dominant detector evidence often comes from residual mismatch and global drift.

## Sensor signature
Look for compact cross-modal inconsistency around the broken edge or head attachment area. Thermal evidence may remain weak, so visual-geometry agreement is the main check.

## Likely root causes
- Impact damage during handling
- Weak bonding at the head or handle junction
- Local plastic fracture under bending stress

## Inspection checks
- Inspect the compact region for edge separation or chipped plastic
- Compare the handle-head outline with a known-good toothbrush
- Check whether the break aligns with a repeated stress point

## Corrective action
Reject the damaged part, inspect the junction or high-stress region, and review handling or bend-load conditions if repeated faults appear.

## Trust notes
Use this guide when the detector identifies compact toothbrush anomalies with strong score-basis evidence and cross-modal inconsistency.
