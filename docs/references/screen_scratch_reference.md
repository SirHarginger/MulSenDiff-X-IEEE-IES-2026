# Screen Scratch Inspection Reference

## Defect pattern
Screen scratches commonly present as compact or narrow anomalies with clear appearance disagreement. Thermal support is often weak, so the detector relies on residual structure and descriptor drift.

## Sensor signature
Focus on compact regions with strong score-basis response and cross-modal inconsistency. Geometry may not dominate, but a scratched screen can still shift the global descriptor distribution away from the normal profile.

## Likely root causes
- Surface abrasion during transport
- Contact with hard tooling or foreign particles
- Cleaning or handling damage on the viewing surface

## Inspection checks
- Inspect the highlighted region under angled lighting
- Verify whether the anomaly aligns with a scratch line or surface abrasion patch
- Compare local surface continuity with a known normal screen

## Corrective action
Segregate scratched units, inspect cleaning and handling stations, and review contact surfaces for abrasive contaminants.

## Trust notes
Use this guide when screen samples are flagged with compact score-basis anomalies and cross-modal inconsistency.
