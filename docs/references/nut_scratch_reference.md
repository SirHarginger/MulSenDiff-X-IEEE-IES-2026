# Nut Scratch and Surface Damage Reference

## Defect pattern
Scratched nut samples often appear as compact anomalies with strong residual disagreement and moderate descriptor drift. The fault may be shallow but still disrupt the normal surface profile.

## Sensor signature
Look for a compact region with mixed cross-modal support. Thermal evidence is typically weak, so the best confirmation comes from local surface deviation and the residual map.

## Likely root causes
- Abrasion from hard contact during transport
- Tool slippage during tightening or placement
- Foreign particles scoring the surface

## Inspection checks
- Inspect the highlighted region for scratch lines or gouges
- Compare the damaged surface to a known-good nut
- Review whether the scratch aligns with tooling or contact direction

## Corrective action
Segregate the part, inspect contact tooling and feeders, and clean any abrasive contamination from the process path.

## Trust notes
This reference supports compact nut scratches where the detector shows cross-modal inconsistency and elevated descriptor drift.
