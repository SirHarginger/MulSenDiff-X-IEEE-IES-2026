# Capsule External Damage Reference

## Defect pattern
Capsule shell damage or broken outside surfaces often produce a compact anomaly with strong detector disagreement and clear cross-modal inconsistency. In challenging cases, the internal-defect rescue pathway may also flag the sample.

## Sensor signature
Prioritize compact regions where appearance disagreement is high but local thermal and geometric support are weak or mixed. Cross-modal inconsistency is especially important for capsule faults that do not present as obvious broad surface damage.

## Likely root causes
- Outer shell fracture from handling impact
- Seal deformation after improper assembly pressure
- Internal structure shift creating inconsistent exterior cues

## Inspection checks
- Examine the compact region for shell cracks or edge breakage
- Check whether neighboring shell geometry remains consistent with a normal capsule
- Confirm whether internal-defect cues are present despite weak broad surface support

## Corrective action
Escalate capsule samples with compact cross-modal inconsistency for manual shell and seal inspection. Review impact handling and assembly pressure history if the same fault repeats.

## Trust notes
This reference is appropriate when the detector reports capsule anomalies with compact cross-modal inconsistency and elevated global descriptor drift.
